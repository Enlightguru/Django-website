About Us

Get to know us

KENYATTA UNIVERSITY STUDENTS AIDS CONTROL ORGANIZATION (KUSACO).

MOTTO.

Encouraging positive behaviour change.

IDENTITY STATEMENT.

Strategic behaviour change.

HISTORY.

Was established in the year 1996 in Kenyatta University

The organization was formed mainly to address the problems of:
1. Drug and substance abuse.
2. Sexual reproductive health.
3. HIV/AIDS and STIs within Kenyatta University community.

GOAL.

To formulate programmes for the management of HIV/AIDS within the university, its neighbourhoods and the entire country as a whole.

MISSION.

To promote activities that lead to a relatively HIV/AIDS free environment while at the same time developing a healthy and productive human resource for the nation.

VISION.

To be a dynamic, competent and highly recognised organization not only within Kenyatta University but also countrywide in fighting against HIV/AIDS.

OBJECTIVES.

a) To initiate and promote activities among the youth which are aimed at the control and prevention of HIV/AIDS.
b) To advocate for positive behaviour change among the youth by use of sustainable and workable multi-face strategies/approaches
c) To form forums to share and exchange ideas, information, skills, knowledge,experience and expertise among students, stakeholders and other parties with similar intrests
d) To initiate individual and institutional capacity building within and outside the university.
e) To create opportunities for interactions amongst the youth through HIV/AIDS exchange program.
f) To instill a sense of responsibility and productivity amongst the youth.
g) To initiate and encourage the formation of youth organizations at community and country level.
h) To initiate an effective policy targeted at fighting the stigma and discrimination in the fight against HIV/AIDS.

ACTIVITIES.

a) Hold workshops and seminars on HIV/AIDS related issues.
b) Visit orpharns and the vulnerable children to offer them support and care.
c) Take part in community services e.g. clean ups and walks.
d) Organise games, entertainment and other fun activities geered towards positive behavioural change.
e) Organising the annual HIV/AIDS Testing Week in conjunction with AIDS Control Unit(ACU) and Kenyatta University Students Association(KUSA).
f) Promotion of talents through participation in the culture week, career weeek's activities.
g) Organizing outreaches on Sexual Reproductive Health(SRH) and HIV/AIDS related issues.
h) Peer education training.
g) Trainers of Trainees (TOT) Trainig.

MEMBERSHIP.

Membership is Open to all Kenyatta University Students who have in them the urge of making a difference and leaving a smile on a neighbours face.
