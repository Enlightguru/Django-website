The Director Kenyatta university Aids Control Unit

Dr. Tabitha Wang'eri

"Thank you for visiting the Kenyatta university Aids control webpage. Here we share our activities and programmes that may be of benefit to you. The various programmes that we undertake aim to enhance progress towards "Zero" that is ... Zero New Hiv infections, Zero Discrimination and Zero AIDs related deaths. Approximately 35 million people are currently living with HIV epidermic in the world. 95% of new infections occur in individuals that reside in low-and middle income countries, particularly in sub-Saharan Africa, Kenya being one of them. HIV primarily affects those in their most productive years. The HIV prevalence among adult ages 15 to 64 decreased from 7.2 percent in 2007 to 5.6 percent in 2012. This represents approximately 1.2 million adults. This is as a result of concerted efforts by the Government of Kenya through the establishment of ACUs and other interventions and the international community which have led to the reduction in the disease burden."
